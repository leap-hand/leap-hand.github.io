LEAP Hand:

Low-Cost, Efficient, and Anthropomorphic Hand for Robot Learning

Kenneth Shaw         Deepak Pathak
Carnegie Mellon University

Abstract

Machine learning has seen widespread progress in many areas of robotics from locomotion to manipulation using grippers, yet research in dexterous manipulation is mostly limited to simulation. This bottleneck can be largely attributed to the absence of affordable, dexterous and durable robotic hand hardware. In this paper, we present LEAP Hand which is designed to be low-cost (less than 2K USD), agile, anthropomorphic, high strength, repeatable, durable, modular, and versatile. We provide an extensive comparison of the strength and efficiency of our LEAP Hand in direct comparison to four other types of multi-fingered hands. We further show that LEAP Hand can be used to perform several manipulation tasks in the real world from visual teleoperation to learning from passive video data. LEAP Hand significantly outperforms its closest competitor Allegro Hand in all the tests while being 1/8th of the cost. Finally, in addition to the real robot, we also release an Isaac Gym and Pybullet based simulator for LEAP Hand. We plan to open-source the URDF model, 3D CAD files, and a development platform with useful APIs upon acceptance.
